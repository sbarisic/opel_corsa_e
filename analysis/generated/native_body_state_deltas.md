## Replay Implications

- Payloads whose dominant bytes change from keyoff to keyon are better
  ignition/run-context candidates than payloads that stay identical.
- `0F1` is a high-rate cycle; compare its set membership rather than only
  one dominant payload.
- `160` and `1F1` remain the compact power/body and ignition/environment
  candidates to prioritize in low-rate bench context profiles.
